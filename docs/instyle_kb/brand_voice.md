## Tone of Voice

### For Customer Service
- **Helpful & patient:** Always assume the customer is new; explain clearly.
- **Warm, not formal:** Use first names; avoid corporate jargon.
- **Action-oriented:** Give clear next steps (link, phone, time).
- **Emoji-friendly:** Use sparingly but meaningfully (‚ú®üíáüèΩ‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏èüî•).

**Example (Good):**
> "Hey babe! üíï Got it‚Äîlet's reschedule you for Saturday at 11 AM. Just click the link in your SMS to confirm, or hit us back if that doesn't work. We've got you!"

**Example (Avoid):**
> "Your appointment modification request has been received. Please utilise the provided link to confirm your rescheduling."

---

### For Marketing & Promotion
- **Aspirational but achievable:** Paint a picture of feeling amazing.
- **Story-driven:** Focus on the transformation, not just the service.
- **Community-celebratory:** Highlight other clients' wins.
- **Authentic:** No false claims; own your niche (hair salon, not tech startup).

**Example (Good):**
> "üåü From TUT campus to client dinners. From 9-to-5 to Friday night out. Your wig installed, your confidence unlocked. Book your Middle & Side now and walk out like you own the room."

**Example (Avoid):**
> "Get a wig install. Fast. Cheap. Now."

## Visual Identity

### Colour Palette
- **Primary:** Crimson Red `#C0392B` ‚Äì bold, confident, luxury
- **Secondary:** Near-Black `#1B1B1B` ‚Äì text, stability, elegance
- **Accent:** Warm Gold `#D4A574` ‚Äì warmth, highlight, premium feel
- **Background:** Off-White `#F9F9F9` ‚Äì clean, modern, accessible

### Typography
- **Headlines:** Bold, modern sans-serif (e.g., Poppins, Inter Bold)
- **Body:** Clean, readable sans-serif (e.g., Poppins Regular, Inter Medium)
- **Accent:** Serif for testimonials (e.g., Georgia) ‚Äì adds prestige

### Logo & Branding
- **Logo Placement:** Sticky header (left), footer, social profiles
- **Logo Size:** 180px width on desktop, 120px on mobile
- **Logo Usage:** Never distort or rotate; white space minimum 20px

## Prohibited Language

- **Avoid corporate jargon:** Not "utilise", "leverage", "synergy"
- **Avoid false claims:** Not "guaranteed perfection" or "lifetime durability"
- **Avoid price-focused messaging:** Not "cheapest in town", "rock-bottom prices"
- **Avoid negative comparisons:** Not "better than SuperSaaS" or "our competition sucks"
